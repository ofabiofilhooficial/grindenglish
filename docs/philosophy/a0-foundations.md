Este curso A0 Foundations foi projetado para brasileiros adultos que querem começar a aprender inglês estudando em um formato híbrido (aulas particulares + prática no seu próprio ritmo). Você aprenderá o "inglês de sobrevivência" essencial para começar a se comunicar imediatamente em situações do dia a dia através de um passo a passo, com explicações claras, glossários em português (pt-BR) e muita prática guiada.
O que você será capaz de fazer ao final do Foundations:
Apresentar-se, fazer perguntas básicas e trocar contatos de WhatsApp/e-mail.
Falar sobre dias e horários, e fazer planos simples.
Descrever um cômodo e localizar objetos (dentro/em cima/embaixo/perto de).
Descrever sua rotina e perguntar sobre a rotina de outras pessoas.
Comprar itens básicos, perguntar preços e ser educado em uma loja.
Pedir e dar direções simples (vire à esquerda/direita, siga em frente).

Como o curso funciona:
6 unidades curtas, cada uma com 4 lições (30 a 60 minutos).
Cada unidade inclui uma tarefa de fala (speaking) + uma tarefa de escrita (writing).
PDFs para imprimir, listas de vocabulário em pt-BR e testes rápidos.
Sessões opcionais de conversação ao vivo para desenvolver a fluência.
Variedade do inglês: Inglês Americano.
Nível recomendado: Iniciante do zero ou "falso iniciante" que precisa de uma base sólida antes de seguir para o nível A1.

Master Scope & Sequence
A0 design note (for planning)
A0 is “foundation survival”: learners can exchange basic info with heavy support, read/write very short messages, and handle predictable micro-transactions.
Recommended A0 unit structure
6 Units
6 lessons per unit (self-paced-ready) + 1 unit performance task (UPA)
Each unit yields:
Speaking artifact (10–60 seconds, gradually increasing)
Writing artifact (form + 1–4 sentences)
Review pack (quiz + vocab list)

Unit-by-unit scope table
A0-U01 — Hello & Identity
Flagship task (real life): Introduce yourself and exchange basic personal info (name, country, job/student, phone/WhatsApp).
Secondary tasks: greetings/leave-taking; spelling your name; confirming information.
Speaking artifact: 30–45s self-introduction recording.
Writing artifact: profile card (name, nationality, city, email/WhatsApp).
Vocabulary focus: greetings; countries/nationalities; jobs; alphabet; numbers 0–20.
Grammar focus: be (affirm/neg/questions); subject pronouns; possessive adjectives.
Pronunciation focus: alphabet letter names; sentence stress on key words; basic question intonation.
PCI focus: greeting norms; polite repetition requests (Sorry? Can you repeat?).
Conversation club link (optional): “Speed introductions” + “Find someone who…” (A0 version).
Lesson sequence
L1 (45m): Greetings + introductions (L1/V)
L2 (45m): Be + personal info Q&A (G/S)
L3 (45m): Alphabet + spelling name/email (V/R1)
L4 (45m): Role-play + profile card + UPA (S/W/F)

A0-U02 — Numbers, Time & Dates
Flagship task: Arrange a simple meeting time/date and confirm details.
Secondary tasks: ask/answer “What time…?”; days/months; simple schedule reading.
Speaking artifact: 45–60s: propose a meeting time + confirm.
Writing artifact: short message: “Hi __. Can we meet on __ at __?”
Vocabulary: numbers 0–100; days; months; times; basic calendar words.
Grammar: What time is it?; It’s…; When…?; on/in/at (intro).
Pronunciation: numbers clarity (15/50; thirteen/thirty); stress in times/dates.
PCI: polite proposing/confirming; “checking” language (So, Tuesday at 5, right?).
Conversation club link: schedule matching game (pair/group).
Lesson sequence
L1 (45m): Numbers 0–100 + phone numbers (V)
L2 (45m): Days/months/dates + “When…?” (V/R1)
L3 (45m): Telling time + “What time…?” (G/S)
L4 (45m): Make plans by text + confirm details + UPA (S/W/F)


A0-U03 — My World (Home & Objects)
Flagship task: Describe your room/home and locate objects (very basic).
Secondary tasks: ask “Where is…?”; understand simple house rules.
Speaking artifact: 30–60s guided description of a room.
Writing artifact: 3–5 sentences: “This is my __. There is/are…”
Vocabulary: rooms; furniture; common objects; colors; basic adjectives (big/small).
Grammar: there is/are; this/that/these/those; basic prepositions (in/on/under/next to).
Pronunciation: /ð/ in this/that (optional); plural -s awareness; chunking with “there is/are”.
PCI: polite asking for help in someone’s home (Where is the bathroom?).
Conversation club link: “Show and tell” (camera or pictures) with

Lesson sequence:

L1 (45m): Rooms/furniture/objects + this/that (V)
L2 (45m): There is/are + simple description (G/W)
L3 (45m): Prepositions of place + “Where is…?” (G/S)
L4 (45m): Show-and-tell (photo/imaginary room) + mini writing + UPA (S/W/F)

A0-U04 — Daily Routines
Flagship task (real life): Talk about your daily routine and ask about someone else’s routine.
Secondary tasks: talk about frequency (simple); basic “what do you do?” Q&A.
Speaking artifact: 45–60s “My day” recording (morning → evening).
Writing artifact: 4–6 sentences: “I get up at… I work/study… I go to bed at…”
Vocabulary focus: routine verbs (wake up, get up, go to work/school, have breakfast, study, exercise, sleep); time phrases (in the morning/at night).
Grammar focus: simple present (I/you/we/they); basic questions with do (intro, controlled); frequency adverbs (always/usually/sometimes—optional A0/A1 bridge).
Pronunciation focus: third-person -s awareness (mostly receptive at A0); sentence stress on time + action; linking in common phrases (get up, go to).
PCI focus: friendly small talk starters/closings; showing interest (“Oh, nice!” “Me too.”).
Conversation club link (recommended): “Daily routine mingle” + “Find someone who…” (very supported).
Lesson sequence 
L1 (45m): Routine verbs + time phrases (V)
L2 (45m): Simple present statements (G)
L3 (45m): Simple questions (Do you…? What do you…?) (G/S)
L4 (45m): Mini-interview + introduce partner + UPA (S/W/F)

A0-U05 — Shopping Basics
Flagship task (real life): Buy items in a store: ask price, quantity, and pay.
Secondary tasks: ask for help finding something; basic preferences (this/that; big/small).
Speaking artifact: 60–90s role-play: customer–cashier OR customer–store assistant.
Writing artifact: a short shopping list (8–12 items) + 2-message chat (“Do you have…?” “How much is…?”).
Vocabulary focus: common food/household items; money; numbers review; containers (a bottle of, a kilo of—very light); store phrases.
Grammar focus: How much…? / How many…? (intro); this/that/these/those (recycle); some (intro as chunk: some water).
Pronunciation focus: numbers + prices clarity; stress in polite requests; plural -s perception in quantities.
PCI focus: polite requesting in service context (Excuse me…, Please, Thank you).
Conversation club link: “Market role-play stations” (rotating roles; fluency-only corrections).
Lesson sequence
L1 (45m): Shopping items + “I’d like…” (V)
L2 (45m): Prices + “How much…?” (G/S)
L3 (45m): Quantities + plural basics + “Do you have…?” (G/S)
L4 (45m): Full role-play purchase + shopping list + UPA (S/W/F)


A0-U06 — Getting Around (Directions & Transport)
Flagship task (real life): Ask for and understand simple directions; identify transport options.
Secondary tasks: landmarks; “near/far”; simple public transport phrases.
Speaking artifact: 60–90s: ask directions + repeat back/confirm route.
Writing artifact: short message: “How do I get to…?” + fill-in directions template (2–4 steps).
Vocabulary focus: places in town (bank, supermarket, pharmacy, bus stop); direction words (left/right/straight); transport (bus, subway—optional, taxi/uber).
Grammar focus: imperatives for directions (turn left, go straight); where is…?; there is/are (recycle for landmarks).
Pronunciation focus: clarity of place names; stress in multiword places (bus stop, train station); intonation for questions.
PCI focus: asking strangers politely; checking understanding (So… left and then right?).
Conversation club link: “Map task” (information gap with two different maps).
Lesson sequence
L1 (45m): Places in town + “Where is…?” (V)
L2 (45m): Imperatives for directions (Turn left…) (G)
L3 (45m): Map task (info-gap) + confirmation checks (S/R1)
L4 (45m): Directions role-play + short written route + UPA (S/W/F)

2. Target Sets (The Language Tools)
| Category | Targeted Items |
| :--- | :--- |
| Lexis | "Hello", "Hi", "Good morning/afternoon", "Name", "First name", "Last name" |
| Upgrades | Basic: Hi, my name is... $\rightarrow$ Professional: Hello, I'm [Name]. Nice to meet you. |
| Grammar | Verb To Be (I am / I'm), Possessive Adjective (My) |
| Pronunciation | Dropping the intrusive Brazilian 'i' (e.g., Good-ee morning-ee $\rightarrow$ Good morning) |
| Pragmatics | Handshake/Nod mechanics and maintaining eye contact during English greetings. |
3. The 50-Minute Execution Flow
Stage 0: Retrieval (5 min): Task: Assess prior knowledge. Write "Oi", "Bom dia", "Boa tarde" on the board. Ask the student for the English equivalents.
Stage 1: The Hook - "Notice the Gap" (7 min): * Scenario: You are logging into a global Zoom meeting for the first time. The host says, "Welcome! Who is joining us?"
Diagnostic Task: Ask the student to introduce themselves immediately.
Anticipated Gap: They will likely use Portuguese pronunciation or forget "Nice to meet you."
Stage 2: Targeted Input (10 min): * The Solution: Present the Grammar Asset: To Be (I am) and Lexicon Asset: Greetings.
Teacher Line: "In a global meeting, we want to sound confident and direct. We use I'm instead of My name is."
Stage 3: Focused Practice (8 min): * Activity: Rapid-fire substitution drill. Teacher says a time (9:00 AM, 3:00 PM), student responds with the correct greeting + I'm [Name].
Stage 4: Communicative Task (15 min): * The Main Event: Zoom Breakout Room Simulation. The student enters 3 different "rooms" (Teacher acts as 3 different personas: a peer, a boss, a client) and must use the appropriate greeting for the time of day and formality.
Stage 5: Debrief & Upgrade (5 min): * Feedback: 1 Strength (Confidence), 1 Fix (Pronunciation of Good), 1 Upgrade (Adding Nice to meet you).
4. Post-Lesson & Differentiation
Homework: Record a 15-second voice note introducing themselves for a Slack/Teams channel.

📚 Lesson 3: Proposing a Plan

| Grammar | Yes/No Questions with To Be (Are you...? / Is the meeting...?) |

Stage 5: Debrief & Upgrade (5 min): * Feedback: Push for the fluid integration of the Upgrade Phrase (Works for me).
4. Post-Lesson & Differentiation
Homework: Write a 3-line email proposing a meeting time to a client using the platform's text editor.

3. The 50-Minute Execution Flow
Stage 0: Retrieval (5 min): TPR Check. Teacher says, "Turn left. Go straight." Student points/gestures the directions.
Stage 1: The Hook (7 min): * Scenario: You are inside a massive US tech conference center. You need to find "Meeting Room B" before your presentation starts. You stop a staff member.
Diagnostic Task: Get their attention and ask for the room.
Anticipated Gap: Saying "Sorry, where is room B?" (Too blunt) or "I want Room B" (Rude).
Stage 2: Targeted Input (10 min): * The Solution: Present Grammar Asset: Present Continuous (Looking for).
Teacher Line: "In the US, 'Sorry' means you made a mistake. 'Excuse me' is how we politely interrupt. Combine that with I am looking for, and you instantly sound like a polite, competent professional."
Stage 3: Focused Practice (8 min): * Activity: The Interruption Drill. Teacher flashes images of locations (an exit, a bathroom, a specific hotel). Student must instantly produce the full string: "Excuse me, I am looking for the [Location]."
Stage 4: Communicative Task (15 min): * The Main Event: "The Conference Maze." The teacher acts as various distracted conference attendees. The student must successfully interrupt them, ask for a specific vendor booth, listen to the directions (L2 recycling), and confirm the route.
Stage 5: Debrief & Upgrade (5 min): * Feedback: Praise the use of Excuse me. Correct the "-ing" pronunciation to ensure it sounds natural.
4. Post-Lesson & Differentiation
Homework: Record a 15-second voice note pretending to be lost in an airport and asking a security guard for the exit.
