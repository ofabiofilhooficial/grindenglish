1. In Unit 1, go to the "Lessons" tab

   - Type: "vocabulary" (or "integrated")
3. Click "Add"
4. Click on the lesson to open the editor

## Step 3: Set Up Lesson Metadata

In the lesson editor:

**Title**: Professional Greetings & Names

**Goal**: 
```
By the end of this lesson, you can confidently greet a colleague and 
introduce your first and last name using the verb "to be" in a 
professional context.
```

**Success Criteria**:
```
You can:
- Use appropriate greetings for different times of day
- Introduce yourself using "I'm [name]"
- Give your first and last name clearly
- Use "Nice to meet you" appropriately
- Pronounce greetings without adding extra vowel sounds
```

**Interaction Pattern**: Solo (or Pair if students work together)

## Step 4: Create Lesson Stages

### Stage 0: Retrieval (5 min)
**Type**: Warm-up  
**Title**: Retrieval - Prior Knowledge

**Instructions**:
```
Think about greetings in Portuguese:
- Oi
- Bom dia
- Boa tarde

Do you know the English equivalents?

Write them down before continuing.
```

**Resources**: None (this is activation)

---

### Stage 1: The Hook - "Notice the Gap" (7 min)
**Type**: Lead-in  
**Title**: The Hook - Notice the Gap

**Instructions**:
```
SCENARIO: You are logging into a global Zoom meeting for the first time.

The host says: "Welcome! Who is joining us?"

TASK: Introduce yourself right now. What would you say?

Think about:
- How do you greet everyone?
- How do you say your name?
- What else should you say?

Try it out loud before moving to the next stage.
```

**Resources**: None (diagnostic task)

---

### Stage 2: Targeted Input (10 min)
**Type**: Input  
**Title**: The Solution - How to Introduce Yourself

**Instructions**:
```
In a global meeting, we want to sound confident and direct.

THE PROFESSIONAL WAY:
"Hello, I'm [First Name] [Last Name]. Nice to meet you."

NOT: "My name is..." (sounds like a child)
USE: "I'm..." (sounds professional)

IMPORTANT: Use the correct greeting for the time of day:
- Before 12:00 PM → "Good morning"
- 12:00 PM - 6:00 PM → "Good afternoon"  
- After 6:00 PM → "Good evening"

PRONUNCIATION TIP:
Don't add extra "i" sounds at the end of words!
❌ "Good-ee morning-ee"
✅ "Good morning"

Study the grammar and vocabulary resources below to understand 
how to use "I'm" and "my" correctly.
```

**Resources to Link**:
- Grammar: A0.G.01 - Verb To Be: I am / I'm
- Grammar: A0.G.02 - Possessive Adjective: My
- Vocabulary: hello
- Vocabulary: good morning
- Vocabulary: good afternoon
- Vocabulary: I'm (if exists, or use grammar)
- Vocabulary: nice to meet you

---

### Stage 3: Focused Practice (8 min)
**Type**: Practice  
**Title**: Rapid-Fire Substitution Drill

**Instructions**:
```
ACTIVITY: Practice greetings for different times of day.

For each time below, say the complete introduction:
[Greeting], I'm [Your Name]. Nice to meet you.

Practice times:
1. 9:00 AM → "Good morning, I'm..."
2. 3:00 PM → "Good afternoon, I'm..."
3. 7:00 PM → "Good evening, I'm..."
4. 10:00 AM → "Good morning, I'm..."
5. 1:00 PM → "Good afternoon, I'm..."

Say each one OUT LOUD. Record yourself if possible.

Focus on:
- Clear pronunciation (no extra vowel sounds!)
- Natural rhythm
- Confident tone
```

**Resources to Link**:
- Vocabulary: good morning
- Vocabulary: good afternoon
- Vocabulary: name
- Vocabulary: first name
- Vocabulary: last name

---

### Stage 4: Communicative Task (15 min)
**Type**: Communicative Use  
**Title**: Zoom Breakout Room Simulation

**Instructions**:
```
THE MAIN EVENT: You will enter 3 different "rooms" and introduce yourself.

ROOM 1: Meeting a Peer (9:00 AM)
Situation: You're joining a team meeting with colleagues at your level.
Task: Greet everyone and introduce yourself.

ROOM 2: Meeting Your Boss (2:00 PM)
Situation: You're in a one-on-one meeting with your manager.
Task: Greet your boss professionally and introduce yourself.

ROOM 3: Meeting a Client (10:00 AM)
Situation: You're in a client call for the first time.
Task: Greet the client and introduce yourself formally.

For each room:
1. Choose the right greeting for the time
2. Introduce yourself: "I'm [First Name] [Last Name]"
3. Add: "Nice to meet you"
4. If with a teacher/partner, they will respond

PRACTICE TIP: If studying alone, record yourself doing all 3 introductions.
```

**Resources to Link**:
- Vocabulary: hello
- Vocabulary: good morning
- Vocabulary: good afternoon
- Vocabulary: nice to meet you

---

### Stage 5: Debrief & Upgrade (5 min)
**Type**: Feedback  
**Title**: Debrief & Upgrade

**Instructions**:
```
REFLECTION: How did you do?

Think about:
1. STRENGTH: What did you do well?
   (Example: I spoke with confidence)

2. FIX: What needs improvement?
   (Example: I need to work on pronouncing "Good" without adding "ee")

3. UPGRADE: What can you add next time?
   (Example: I can add "It's a pleasure to meet you" for very formal situations)

PROFESSIONAL UPGRADE:
Basic: "Hi, my name is Carlos."
Professional: "Hello, I'm Carlos Silva. Nice to meet you."
Advanced: "Good morning, I'm Carlos Silva. It's a pleasure to meet you."

Write down your personal upgrade for next time.
```

**Resources to Link**:
- Grammar: A0.G.01 - Verb To Be: I am / I'm
- Vocabulary: nice to meet you

## Homework (Post-Lesson)

Add this to the lesson's homework field:

```
HOMEWORK: Professional Introduction Recording

Record a 15-second voice note introducing yourself as if you're 
joining a Slack or Teams channel for the first time.

Include:
- Appropriate greeting for the current time
- Your full name using "I'm..."
- "Nice to meet you" or similar phrase

Submit your recording in the assignments section.

BONUS: Record yourself at 3 different times of day (morning, afternoon, evening) 
and use the appropriate greeting for each.
```

## Troubleshooting

**"Can't link assets"**
→ Make sure you ran all 3 migrations

**"Grammar/vocabulary not found"**
→ Run migration 20260306000004_seed_lesson_1_professional_greetings.sql

**"Assets not showing in lesson"**
→ Make sure you clicked "Save" after linking
→ Check that grammar/vocabulary is published
→ Refresh the page

**"Can't see unit targets"**
→ Run migration 20260306000003_create_unit_assets.sql
→ Make sure AssetSelector is imported in UnitEditorPage
